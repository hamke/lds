Cutting-Edge Coffee

July 13, 2014 / Coppell Gazette

John Barry thinks people are ready for a change – at least when it comes to their coffeehouse choice. In the United States, national brands like Starbucks and Dunkin Donuts dominate the landscape, with mom and pop stores filling out the rest, Barry said, He’s hoping that with Caffebene combining the best aspects of both of those types of coffee shops that DFW coffee lovers will have a new choice.

“Our look is very different, “ said Barry, director of U.S. development and franchising for Caffebene. “We want to be in every community and be the coffeehouse of choice. It’s a great concept, and the time is right for it. It’s time for a new generation to experience a new company that’s cutting edge in truly what they deserve.”

Caffebene opened in Carrollton in April, marking it the first coffeehouse for the company in Texas. Caffebene is mainly an international coffee shop popular in Korea. It didn’t debut in the U.S until 2012 with a shop in New York. Now the company is looking to expand, and Barry said Carrollton just turned out to be a perfect match.

“Demographically it worked for us.” Barry said. “We’re 20 miles outside of Dallas on the north side, so we like the population base.” Barry said there are four things that help Caffebene stand out among the big competitors like Starbucks and also the smaller, local shops – the look of the store, its service, its loyalty program and it attracts the younger generation.

The shops are modeled after old European shops. It’s a distinct look that Barry said should attract plenty of new customers looking for a bit of a change. “It’s where writers and philosophers got together to preach ideas. It’s an informal and very relaxed setting.” For the service, Barry said the shop schedules employees to work the same shift, as to better build rapport with regular customers. That service also extends to handing out free samples while customers stand inn line and what Barry said are only the freshest ingredients.

“There are relationships that we’re trying to develop,” he said. “We’re trying to bring a service level to the coffeehouse level that does not exist at Starbucks and mom and pops.” While Barry encourages everyone to go Caffebene, he said that targeting ages 18-33 is a big part of what they’re doing. Barry said it’s not just about the look and style of the shop. He said the company did tons of research to find out what this age group liked and didn’t like. “That group will represent about 50 percent of workforce by 2020,” Barry said. “We found out this group eats little meals each day and tend to have sporadic meals. There’s lot of things we researched.” That extends to the menu options as the store offers waffles, pastries, sandwiches, parfaits and gelato.

Barry said the Carrollton location though has some more Hispanic influenced items to better represent the North Texas population. As for the loyalty program, Barry said he couldn’t go into details about it just yet but said it’ll be more than the standard buy such as buy a certain number drinks, get the next one free.

Being a new name, Barry said the understands it will be a slow rise to change consumers’ opinions. With the location in Carrollton up and running, the next planned site is for Houston. Barry hopes it’ll spread even more after that. He thinks it’s time for customers in Carrollton to have more choices.

“Starbucks has been around 40 years and they’ve done a great job, it’s a wonderful company,” he said. “But they’re like an old pair of Nike sneakers. We want something new, we want something current.”
