Korean Starbucks Heads to Long Island

May 30, 2014 / Long Island Business News

One of the world’s fastest-growing coffee shop chains is coming to Long Island as part of a major U.S. expansion.

Caffebene, a South Korea-based coffee franchise with about 1,500 stores across Asia and Europe, has just 13 locations in the United States, but plans 83 more here by the end of the year. Many of those will be in the New York area, including one in Great Neck. The chain has signed deals with more than 80 new franchises in the five boroughs and is projected to have 500 stores in New York City by the end of 2015, which would make Caffebene the city’s third-largest chain.

Sometimes called the Korean Starbucks, Caffebene has grown dramatically since its first store opened in Seoul in 2008. The company is planning to have 10,000 outlets worldwide by 2020. Its first store in this area – a 6,000 square-foot flagship – was opened in Times Square in February 2012.

Mi-Kyung Shin is Long Island’s pioneer Caffebene franchise. Originally from Seoul but now living in Flushing, Shin was attracted to the coffee concept because it’s a business that can cater to everyone.

“I thought that franchising would be a better fit because I have not had previous experience with opening a restaurant,” Shin said via email.

All-in costs for a Caffebene franchise ranges from $413,000 to $799,000, depending on location and size. The company is currently seeking franchises for Long Island, to own 60 future locations in Nassau and Suffolk. The ideal spaces are around 3,000 square feet with good visibility and ample parking.

Shin liked the spot at 1 South Middle Neck Road for this area’s first Caffebene because of its proximity to the Long Island Rail Road station, bus stops and other retailers.
